About Sinhagad Fort

Perched atop a steep hill, Sinhagad Fort is a historic stronghold near Pune that dates back to over 2,000 years. Originally known as Kondhana, it has witnessed countless battles, including the legendary battle fought by Tanaji Malusare during Shivaji Maharaj's reign. This fort is celebrated for its strategic importance, stunning views, and deep connection to Maratha history.

History

Sinhagad Fort, located 35 kilometers southwest of Pune, holds immense historical and cultural significance. It served as a critical defense point for the Maratha Empire, standing guard against invading forces. The fort is most renowned for the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670, where the brave warrior Tanaji Malusare sacrificed his life to reclaim the fort for Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. His valor is immortalized in the Marathi phrase, "Gad ala pan Sinha gela," meaning "The fort is won, but the lion is lost."
