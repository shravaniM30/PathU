About Parvati Hill

Parvati Hill is one of the oldest and highest points in Pune, offering a breathtaking view of the city. The hill is home to the Parvati Temple, which was built in the 18th century and is dedicated to Lord Shiva, Parvati, and other deities. The scenic beauty and historical significance make it a popular spot for pilgrims and tourists alike.

Highlights

Parvati Hill is a historical and religious site offering stunning views and tranquil surroundings. Some of the key highlights include:

Key Features:

  • **Historical Temples:** The Parvati Temple and its smaller shrines dedicated to other gods.
  • **Panoramic Views:** The hilltop provides a panoramic view of Pune city and surrounding areas, especially stunning during sunrise and sunset.
  • **Ancient Architecture:** The temples on the hill are built in traditional Maratha architecture, with intricate carvings and sculptures.
  • **Climbing Experience:** The hill has 103 steps leading to the top, offering a peaceful and meditative climb for visitors.
  • **Rich Cultural Heritage:** The site has religious significance for Hindus and offers a glimpse into Pune's history and heritage.
The hill is a perfect destination for nature lovers, trekkers, and those seeking spiritual peace amidst stunning views.

Visit Us

Parvati Hill is open to visitors throughout the year, and the best time to visit is during the early morning or evening for the most pleasant experience and stunning views of the city. The hill is easily accessible by road, and the climb to the temple is a must for anyone looking to enjoy a peaceful yet enriching experience.

Timings: Open daily from 5:00 AM to 8:00 PM
