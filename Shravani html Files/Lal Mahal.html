Lal Mahal

Step into the history of Pune with a visit to the iconic Lal Mahal, a symbol of the Maratha Empire's legacy.

About Lal Mahal

The Lal Mahal, or "Red Palace," is one of Pune's most significant historical landmarks. Originally built in 1630 by Shahaji Bhosale, it served as the residence of Jijabai and young Shivaji Maharaj. It is the place where Shivaji spent his formative years and planned the foundation of the Maratha Empire. Today, the reconstructed Lal Mahal stands as a tribute to that glorious past, showcasing artifacts and paintings that narrate tales of bravery and valor.

Highlights

When you visit Lal Mahal, here’s what you can explore:

  • Historical Significance: Learn about the early life of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and his strategic brilliance.
  • Artifacts and Paintings: Admire the artwork and displays depicting key events from Maratha history.
  • Jijamata Garden: A tranquil space that adds to the charm of the Mahal.

The Mahal’s vibrant red structure and detailed interiors are a visual delight, making it a must-visit for history enthusiasts and travelers alike.

Location

Lal Mahal is located in the heart of Pune, near Shaniwar Wada, making it easily accessible for tourists.
