Pataleshwar Cave Temple

Step into ancient history with a visit to the Pataleshwar Cave Temple, Pune's architectural and spiritual marvel.

About Pataleshwar Cave Temple

The Pataleshwar Cave Temple, carved out of a single basalt rock in the 8th century, is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Located in the heart of Pune, this ancient temple showcases exquisite rock-cut architecture and intricate carvings. The temple's serene ambiance and historical significance make it a popular spot for both devotees and history enthusiasts.

Highlights

Some key highlights of the Pataleshwar Cave Temple include:

  • Rock-Cut Architecture: The temple is an engineering marvel with intricate carvings and monolithic structures.
  • Shivalinga: A sacred Shivalinga housed within the main sanctum, drawing devotees from near and far.
  • Nandi Mandapa: A beautifully carved pavilion housing the idol of Nandi, Lord Shiva’s vahana.

The temple’s calm environment amidst the bustling city offers a perfect retreat for meditation and relaxation.

Location

The Pataleshwar Cave Temple is located on Jangali Maharaj Road, making it easily accessible from anywhere in Pune.

Visit Us

The Pataleshwar Cave Temple is open throughout the year and is best visited during the mornings or evenings for a serene experience.

Timings: 8:00 AM to 5:30 PM
