About Aga Khan Palace

Aga Khan Palace, located in Pune, Maharashtra, is a magnificent historical landmark built in 1892 by Sultan Muhammad Shah Aga Khan III. Known for its impressive architecture, lush gardens, and rich historical significance, the palace played a key role during India’s freedom struggle. It served as a detention center for Mahatma Gandhi, Kasturba Gandhi, and other leaders during the Quit India Movement.

History

The Aga Khan Palace was built as an act of charity to provide employment to the famine-stricken people of Pune during the late 19th century. Over the years, it became a prominent symbol of India’s struggle for independence. Mahatma Gandhi and his wife Kasturba Gandhi were imprisoned here by the British during the Quit India Movement in 1942. The palace holds a deep historical significance, as Kasturba Gandhi and Mahadev Desai, Gandhi’s secretary, passed away within its walls.


Today, the palace stands as a memorial to Mahatma Gandhi, housing a museum with rare photographs, personal belongings, and a room preserved as a tribute to the Father of the Nation. Its beautifully landscaped gardens and serene environment attract tourists, historians, and peace seekers from across the globe.

Visit Us

The Aga Khan Palace is open to visitors throughout the year. It offers a tranquil environment and a glimpse into India’s freedom struggle, making it a must-visit destination in Pune. The palace also hosts annual events commemorating Mahatma Gandhi, including his death anniversary.
